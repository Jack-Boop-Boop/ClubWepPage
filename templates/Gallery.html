Neutral Net

By Clayton Durepos

Neutral Net is an AI-powered tool designed to search for news articles and analyze them for bias. Developed by Clayton Durepos for the COS398 Ethics and Social Impacts of Computing course at the University of Southern Maine, the application utilizes SerpAPI to retrieve Google search results and employs a fine-tuned BERT model to assess and score each article's bias. The bias scores range from 0 to 100, with lower scores indicating more factual content and higher scores suggesting greater bias. This tool aims to help combat the spread of misinformation by providing users with insights into the bias of news articles.
